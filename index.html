Corpora and Diachrony

Influential Texts, Text Types, and Genres

International Workshop

November 27-29, 2025 European Cultural Centre of Delphi, Greece

About the Workshop

This international workshop brings together leading scholars in corpus linguistics and historical language studies to explore the role of influential texts, text types, and genres in language change. The workshop is part of the HFRI/ELIDEK funded research project "Genres, text types, influential texts: Their role in language change."

The workshop focuses on two main themes:

  • The development of corpus-based valency lexicons for contrastive and diachronic studies of ancient and medieval languages
  • The impact of genres, text types, and influential texts on patterns of language change

All participants receive full financial support through HFRI/ELIDEK funding, covering accommodation, meals, and travel expenses for invited speakers.

Keynote Speakers

Prof. Dag Trygve Truslew Haug

University of Oslo, Norway

Leading expert in dependency grammar and treebank development. Director of the PROIEL treebank project, focusing on parallel ancient texts and historical syntax.

Profile

Prof. George Mikros

Hamad Bin Khalifa University, Qatar

Computational linguist specializing in stylometry and authorship attribution. Extensive work on Greek text analysis using quantitative methods.

Profile

Prof. Ioanna Sitaridou

University of Cambridge, UK

Expert in Greek dialectology and syntactic variation. Principal investigator of the Romeyka Project, documenting endangered Greek varieties.

Profile

Dr. Alexander Tantos

Aristotle University of Thessaloniki, Greece

Corpus linguist focusing on Modern Greek corpus development. Creator of multiple Greek language resources and computational tools.

Profile

Prof. Carola Trips

University of Mannheim, Germany

Expert in diachronic syntax and language contact. Specializes in the impact of French on Middle English and syntactic borrowing in medieval texts.

Venue

European Cultural Centre of Delphi

The workshop will be held at the European Cultural Centre of Delphi, a prestigious venue located in the historic town of Delphi, Greece. The Centre provides state-of-the-art conference facilities in a setting of exceptional cultural significance.

Accommodation

Participants will be accommodated at the European Cultural Centre Guesthouse, offering comfortable single rooms with breakfast included. The guesthouse is within walking distance of all conference facilities.

Getting There

Organized transportation will be provided from Athens to Delphi on November 26 (afternoon departure) and return transportation on November 29 (after lunch). The journey takes approximately 2.5 hours.
